Mamoru Oshii

Creator of Influential Contemporary Multimedia

Mamoru Oshii's works are stylistically dark and laden with political or psychological undertones, fitting right along side the likes of other well known and distintcive works such as Blade Runner (1982). Arguably most well known for his highly popular and influential Anime movie Ghost in the Shell (1995), has had deffinitive influence across multimedia franchises such as The Matrix [a 1999 movie], and even the video game Oni (1998), some of Mamoru Oshii's more esoteric works are truly remarkable and noir. One such work, the Kerberos Saga (ケルベロス・サーガ Keruberosu saga), is ongoing but encompasses multiple forms of media including books, film, animation, and radio. Set in an alternate history universe it tells the story of a secret military police unit, with uniforms emblazened with the mythological three-headed dog known for guarding the gate to Hell, whose objective is to identify and eliminate terrorist and anti-government organizations and their affiliates.

I particularly find this series an interesting work of Mamoru Oshii because its spread among so many varied mediums to tell the story. I believe this not only extends the bounds of the possible target audience, but also provides additional vectors of immersion into the story for the audience.
